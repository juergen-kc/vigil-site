What it does

  • A dozen live data layers on a 3D globe: weather, earthquakes, live flights, news, air quality, weather radar, traffic, calendar, and more.
  • Each layer renders as pins, decals, polylines, pulse rings, or heatmap billboards — chosen for the data, not the framework.
  • Tap any feature to fly the globe to it, open a detail window, and read AI-generated context.
  • Ambient mode auto-rotates the globe and cycles through your saved watchlist locations — a glanceable wall display.
  • On-device AI briefings & chat — ask about your globe, powered by Apple’s built-in intelligence. No account, no API key, works offline.

What it doesn’t do

  • No accounts. No login screen. No telemetry.
  • No remote sync of your watchlist or chat history (CloudKit container is reserved for v1.1).
  • No third-party SDKs that send data anywhere.
  • No subscription — Vigil is a one-time $4.99 purchase on the visionOS App Store, every layer and feature included.

How to get it

Mac. The macOS app is free during the public beta. Download the latest signed + notarized build from GitHub Releases. Updates arrive automatically via Sparkle.

System requirements

  • macOS Vigil: macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later. Apple Silicon or Intel.
  • visionOS Vigil: Apple Vision Pro running visionOS 26 or later.
  • Optional: WeatherKit entitlement (active Apple Developer account) for the Weather layer when building from source.
